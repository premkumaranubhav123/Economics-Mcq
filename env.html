Biodiversity Hotspot Criteria
≥1500 endemic vascular plant species (irreplaceable)
Must have lost ≥70% of original habitat
36 global hotspots (as of 2023)
India has 4: Western Ghats, Eastern Himalayas, Indo-Burma, Sundaland
UPSC TRAP: Indo-Burma includes NE India (not just Myanmar)
IUCN Red List — Key Categories
EX → Extinct (no individuals anywhere)
EW → Extinct in Wild (only in captivity)
CR → Critically Endangered (highest threat)
EN → Endangered
VU → Vulnerable
Mnemonic: Every Exhausted Creature Eventually Vanishes
Ramsar Convention Essentials
Signed: 1971 (Iran); Entered force: 1975
India joined: 1982
Chilika Lake: India's 1st Ramsar site (1981)
UPSC TRAP: Ramsar ≠ UNESCO (separate body)
Montreux Record = threatened Ramsar sites
Paris Agreement Key Points
Adopted: 2015 (Paris, COP21)
Limit warming: 1.5°C (well below 2°C)
NDCs = Nationally Determined Contributions
Loss & Damage fund established at COP27
TRAP: Paris is under UNFCCC, not CBD
Mangroves — UPSC Favorite Points
Grow in brackish/tidal water (saline + fresh)
Pneumatophores = aerial roots for O₂
Vivipary = seeds germinate on parent plant
India: Sundarbans = largest mangrove globally
TRAP: Mangroves ≠ exclusive freshwater forests
Ecological Pyramid Rules
Pyramid of Numbers — can be inverted (tree ecosystem)
Pyramid of Biomass — can be inverted (aquatic)
Pyramid of Energy — ALWAYS upright (UPSC trap!)
10% law: only 10% energy transfers per trophic level
Mnemonic: NBE — Numbers+Biomass can invert, Energy never
